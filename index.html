How It Works

Securing the Referral-to-Report Journey

Every diagnostic test order passes through NexusDiag AI's four-stage intelligence pipeline — converting fragmented hospital data into real-time workflow oversight, predictive alerts, and closed-loop clinical notifications.

STEP 01

HL7/FHIR Data Ingestion

The platform connects seamlessly to existing LIMS, RIS, and EPR systems via HL7/FHIR R5 APIs — creating a unified patient diagnostic timeline without requiring hardware changes or new infrastructure investment.

STEP 02

Predictive Bottleneck Detection

The hybrid CNN-RNN engine analyses diagnostic throughput in real time, identifying tests at risk of breaching the 6-week NHS standard before delays occur — enabling proactive resource reallocation with sub-200ms latency.

STEP 03

AI Clinical Validation (NLP)

The NLP layer cross-references laboratory findings with original referral notes, flagging interpretive inconsistencies — such as "normal" results representing significant deviations from a patient's historical baseline — for immediate secondary review.

STEP 04

Closed-Loop Notification & Audit

Critical findings instantly notify referring clinicians via secure HL7/FHIR channels, bypassing administrative delays. Every interaction is recorded on an encrypted, legally defensible audit trail — reducing clinical negligence liability for NHS Trusts.

FAQ

Common Questions

What exactly is the "Information Silo" problem that NexusDiag AI solves?

+
The Information Silo refers to the systemic disconnect between GPs ordering diagnostic tests, laboratory scientists processing them, and radiologists or clinicians reporting results. Currently, LIMS and PACS systems act as isolated data repositories that record information but do not "bridge" communication between departments. This means 24% of Missed Diagnostic Opportunities (MDOs) occur simply because results are never followed up — affecting 4.3% of all GP consultations, equating to 6 million consultations annually. NexusDiag AI closes this gap by operating as an interoperability middleware layer that synchronizes every step of the diagnostic journey in real time, ensuring no result is ever left unread or unactioned.

How does the Predictive Workflow Intelligence Engine prevent 6-week target breaches?

+
Traditional LIMS systems alert clinical staff after a delay has already occurred — by which point a breach is often inevitable. NexusDiag AI's hybrid CNN-RNN architecture analyses historical throughput data and current capacity simultaneously, calculating the "velocity" of each diagnostic sample or scan through the system. If a department's processing speed drops below a critical threshold for specific biopsy types or imaging modalities, the engine generates automated alerts in under 200ms — allowing managers to reallocate radiologists, additional scanners, or processing staff before the delay breaches the NHS 6-week constitutional standard. This shifts management from reactive to proactive, directly addressing the 46% of NHS Trusts currently missing interim diagnostic targets.

Does NexusDiag AI require replacing existing LIMS or PACS infrastructure?

+
No. NexusDiag AI is specifically designed as a software-first middleware overlay that integrates with existing systems via HL7/FHIR R5 open standards — the same protocols already used by Epic, Cerner, EMIS, and SystmOne. No "rip and replace" of existing LIMS, RIS, or EPR systems is required. The platform functions as a "Universal Handshake" layer that creates a unified view across disparate hospital IT systems without requiring capital-intensive infrastructure changes. For institutions requiring edge processing, a compact secure diagnostic gateway unit is available for local de-identification, but the core SaaS platform deploys entirely via cloud APIs.

How does NexusDiag AI comply with NHS DTAC 2.0 and GDPR for patient data?

+
NexusDiag AI is specifically pre-aligned with DTAC Version 2, which became mandatory from 6 April 2026. The platform processes all sensitive patient metadata locally using Edge AI gateways within hospital firewalls — ensuring no Patient Identifiable Information (PII) is ever transmitted to external cloud servers. All cloud processing uses Microsoft Azure UK South data centres with AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit. The platform includes a full DCB0129 Clinical Safety Case with a registered Clinical Safety Officer (CSO), Hazard Log, and Safety Case Report. The NLP clinical validation engine undergoes biannual bias audits to ensure consistent performance across patient demographics in line with NHS Core20PLUS5 health equity standards.

Will the platform affect diagnostic workflow performance or radiologist workload?

+
No. NexusDiag AI is specifically engineered to augment, not replace, clinical decision-making. Hospitals using AI-supported diagnostics see a 42% reduction in errors and up to 35% overall efficiency improvement — reclaiming millions of hours of clinical time previously spent on administrative tracking and manual result distribution. The platform operates with sub-200ms latency, meaning its intelligent alerts and validation prompts appear seamlessly within existing clinical worklists without adding cognitive load. The human-in-the-loop framework ensures all automated tags and tactical suggestions are subject to clinician review, maintaining human agency in the diagnostic process as required by the EU AI Act 2024 High-Risk AI framework.

The functional prototype has undergone rigorous testing in high-fidelity simulated clinical environments including high-volume phlebotomy units, imaging departments, and Community Diagnostic Centres. Validated results include 92% accuracy in identifying diagnostic workflow delays, 94% precision in report inconsistency flagging, sub-200ms alert propagation, and 95% functionality in offline mode.
